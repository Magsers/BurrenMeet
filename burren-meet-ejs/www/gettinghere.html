Getting Here / Directions

Directions from Cork

Take the N20 to Limerick. After 55km there will be an exit onto the M20 motorway. At junction 1, exit onto N18 toward Galway/Limerick. Exit the motorway after 22km and follow the signs towards Ennistymon. After Ennistymon turn left for Fanore after 10km. This will bring you right into the heart of the Burren where you will see large cliffs to your right. There are numerous parking spots on the right and left of the road. Ballyryan is visible on the right side of the road which in good weather will be heavily populated with climbers. Estimated travel time : 2 hours 25 minutes

Directions from Dublin

Follow the M6 towards Galway until you reach Junction 18. At Junction 18 exit onto the M18 towards Limerick. Follow the coastal route towards Kinvara, Ballyvaughan and Fanore. As you drive past Fanore the sea will be on your right. After two miles of stunning scenery you will see the unmistakeable Ballyryan climbing cliff on your left where you can park. Estimated travel time : 2 hours 50 minutes

Directions from Galway

Follow the N67 coastal road from Galway to Kinvara. After Kinvara follow signs for Ballyvaughan and Fanore. After two miles of stunning scenery you will see the unmistakeable Ballyryan climbing cliff on your left where you can park.Estimated travel time : 1 hour 10 minutes.

It is well known that coffee and climbing are two sides of the same coin, coffee makes climbing better and climbing enhances your coffee experience. We have organised for our very own coffee slave, Dave, to come and brew our coffees from morning until late afternoon daily from his mobile coffee van. Make sure to bring your own cup and some coinage as it is cash only. "Quirky, quick and quality! Best coffee I've had in years" Coffee Connioisseur

Meal Deal

There is plenty options to choose from for dining but the Doolin Hotel have kindly offered to do Fish and Chips for climbers attending the festival for a discounted rate of €10 with a pint included. This offer is available from Friday to Sunday night. Just use the enquiries email address on the Contact Us page to reserve a token for you.

Shuttle Bus

There will be a shuttle bus running from the crag to Doolin Friday, Saturday and Sunday from 6pm to ferry climbers to the village for an evening meal and for the talks. This is to ensure people can have a drink and not worry about driving or parking their car again as parking can be tight and queueing to park causes traffic jams. We really want to keep congestion by the crag to a minimum. The cost for the shuttle bus is €5 return and you can just pay the driver.
